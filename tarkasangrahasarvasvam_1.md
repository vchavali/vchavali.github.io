The Tarkasangrahasarvasvam (TSS) by Śrī Kurugaṇṭi Rāmaśāstri is a detailed commentary on Annambhaṭṭa’s famous introductory textbook, the Tarkasangrahaḥ (TS). The TSS is special as a commentary because its aim is not simply to explain the text as with, e.g., the Dīpika (another famous commentary on the same text). Rather, its goal is to teach the analytical techniques, formalisms, and abstractions of Navyanyāya using Annambhaṭṭa’s root text as a model for demonstration.

The TS a a collection of definitions of the fundemental Nyāya concepts in ontology, epistemology, and dialectics. The original text presents these definitions in natural language in a simple style aimed at being a sort of sourcebook or handbook for beginners. Hence the name, which translates to something like "Handbook of Philosophy". 

The TSS takes the definitions found in the TS and formalizes them using the machinery of Navyanyāya. In addition, it provides detailed, step-by-step instructions for how the formalisms are derived and why they have the form that they have. So, its an amazing guide for learnng about these formalisms and how to use, parse, and reason about them.

I’ve been working through this text and the material below collects my notes and thoughts on this material. Its very much a work in progress, but maybe it would will be of some use to others as well!
